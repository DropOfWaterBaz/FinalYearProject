ENFJs or Protagonists are passionate and charismatic people. They have strong leadership skills that they use in conjunction with enthusiasm. They are not afraid of speaking out for what they believe needs to be said.

Strengths vs. Weaknesses

Strengths

- Selflessness: Protagonists are generous people who like to contribute to the community around them. They are warm and caring.

- Peacekeeper: Using their strong leadership qualities, they can diffuse conflict with powerful persuasion and good communication.

- Reliabilty: ENFJs are so reliable because they hate the thought of letting someone else down.

Weaknesses

- Decisiveness: A tough decision can leave protagonists feeling paralysed, overthinking the consequences of either outcome of their choice.

- Self-Esteem: ENFJs hold themselves to a high standard, and they're self-esteem is majorly dependent on whether or not they reach these standards.

- Sensitivity: ENFJs accept criticism as it can be used to improve themselves and those around them, but they may take it too seriously. They can think too much about a critique and upset themselves by doing so.

Careers

Protagonists are fantastic at rallying people together to make a cooperative effort as a team. They can delegate, supervise and steer others in the right direction. They keep harmony amongst the group they are in.

ENFJs are certainly suited to a leadership role in a managerial, administrative or political position.

Relationships

ENFJs are very active in their relationships, putting in effort to maintaining their connections with those who matter most to them. They see their relationships as hugely important. They are sincere and are genuinely interested in their friends feelings and aspirations.
